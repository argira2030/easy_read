📱 Attention Protocol
Easy Read Version

This program helps you use your phone with more control. You don't need willpower. Just follow these steps.

1. Change your environment

⬜ Turn off notifications from apps

Notifications steal your attention. Keep only calls and messages from real people.

⬜ Leave your phone outside the room when you work or sleep

Just seeing your phone tires your brain. Keep it far away.

⬜ Reduce screen colors

Bright colors grab your attention. Try these:
- Lower screen brightness.
- Turn on "night mode" or "reading mode".
- If possible, use grayscale (Settings → Accessibility).

❓ Why does seeing your phone tire you even if you don't use it?

Because it makes me anxious
Because my brain uses energy to ignore it
✅ Correct. Your brain uses resources to avoid looking at the phone. That's why you feel tired.

2. Help your brain recover

⬜ Stop endless scrolling

Short videos and endless news are like candy for your brain. Replace them with reading a book or walking without music.

⬜ Allow yourself to be bored

Boredom is normal. Don't cover it with your phone. Look out the window or count to 50.

⬜ Look at sunlight when you wake up

Natural light helps your sleep and mood. Before using your phone, look outside for 5 minutes.

❓ What does it mean to feel empty when you stop scrolling?

That I am addicted
That my brain is regulating itself
✅ That feeling is good. It means your brain is recovering. It goes away after a few days.

3. The power of pause

⬜ Step 1: Notice

Observe that you opened the app without thinking. Don't be angry. Just say: "That was automatic."

⬜ Step 2: Wait 5 seconds

Keep the app open but don't touch anything. Count 5 seconds. This gives your brain time to brake.

⬜ Step 3: Decide

Do you really need to go in or was it just habit? If habit, close the app. If useful, continue with intention.

❓ Why is feeling guilty bad?

Because guilt is useless
Because guilt uses the same energy you need for self-control
✅ Exactly. If you get angry at yourself, you have less energy for next time. Better to observe without judgment.

4. Use your phone as a tool

⬜ Before touching your phone, say out loud what you will do

Example: "I'll check the weather and then close it." This activates your conscious brain.

⬜ Treat your phone like a hammer

You pick up a hammer to drive a nail and then put it down. Do the same with your phone.

⬜ When you finish, say "completed"

This small ritual reinforces using your phone with a purpose and then letting it go.

❓ What's the difference between using the phone as a tool or as a home?

The time you spend
The intention when you pick it up
✅ Exactly. If you know what you're going to do, it's a tool. If you go in without a plan, you get trapped.

📅 30-day calendar

Important days:
✅ Day 7: you start noticing changes.
✅ Day 14: emptiness decreases.
✅ Day 30: your brain no longer needs the phone to feel good.
